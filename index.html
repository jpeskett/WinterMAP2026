Bottom Line for Leadership 196 students failed to reach 2x growth. The data reveals a systemic crisis: 57 students were trapped in doom loops, taking the same test 4+ times while cycling through ineffective content. • Bucket 1 (19%, 37 students) — Curriculum Not Effective: Curriculum doesn't teach what tests measure. Fix: Adopt Direct Instruction curriculum, audit TEKS coverage, ensure 25 min/day on-task time. • Bucket 2 (23%, 45 students) — Curriculum Not Efficient: Students enrolled but never reach the test. Fix: Implement pacing alerts, enforce semester-based completion SLAs, flag supplementary-only enrollments. • Bucket 3a (29%, 57 students) — Tests Not Rigorous Enough: Passed Alpha at 90%+ in 3 or fewer attempts but didn't grow 2x on MAP. Fix: Upgrade K-2 test rigor, add MAP-aligned validation layer for Grade 3+. • Bucket 3b (29%, 57 students) — Doom Loop Crisis: Took 4+ attempts on the same test, cycling through Freckle/Edia with undiagnosed gaps. 47% have HM ≤ 2 (fluency issues). Fix: Deploy doom loop detection, diagnostic-first intervention, replace with Direct Instruction for foundational gaps. The immediate priority: Detect and interrupt doom loops. Then systematically replace low-effectiveness tools and enforce fluency prerequisites. The path forward is not more testing; it's smarter systems, earlier alerts, and deliberate intervention on the patterns that derail growth.
