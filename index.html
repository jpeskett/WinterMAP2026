Key Findings

  • 58% of non-growing students PASSED our internal Alpha tests at 90%+ — this is the single most alarming signal. It means our tests give false confidence about student readiness.
  • 45 students (23%) never even took a mastery exam — they are stuck in the pipeline, enrolled in courses but never reaching the assessment gate.
  • 37 students (19%) tried the test but couldn't pass — the curriculum isn't teaching what the test measures, or students are being tested at the wrong level.
  • Alpha School Austin dominates Bucket 2 with 25 of 45 students — a campus-specific efficiency problem.
  • Average growth across all 196 students is ~1.05x — essentially flat, barely above typical growth, and far below the 2x target.
  • Deep dive students (Lydia Cobas, George Troxell, Elle Zak, Lucius Walker) are all in Bucket 3, confirming the systemic pattern.

Bucket 1: Curriculum Not Effective (37 students)

These students took the Effective Grade Level test but FAILED to pass at 90%. They put in effort — they attempted the assessment — but the curriculum did not prepare them to succeed. Why didn't the curriculum work?

  • Standards not covered at the right depth or rigor — content was taught but not to the depth MAP requires
  • Standards missing from content entirely — specific TEKS were not included in the curriculum
  • Students need more practice — insufficient repetition and application to build mastery
  • Insufficient time on task — compare these students to those who DID achieve 2x growth; time investment falls short
  • Poor foundational curriculum — Early exposure to ineffective tools like Synthesis Tutor (K-3, representing 15% of instructional tool this year) weakens the foundation

Key Patterns

  • Average growth: 0.95x — essentially flat. 13 students were between 1.5x-2.0x, meaning they were growing but still missed the 90% pass mark.
  • 41% are in Zearn courses, 35% in Math Academy — the curriculum source doesn't seem to be the differentiator; the gap is in depth of mastery.
  • Several students like Elijah Knecht (89.3%), Sofia Page (88.2%), and Ozzie Lowe (86.1%) are within striking distance of 90% — targeted intervention could push them over.
  • Others like Eastwood Washburn (16%), Vivian Ajouz (20%), and Brooke Tracy (43.8%) have fundamental gaps — they may be placed at the wrong level entirely.

Bucket 2: Curriculum Not Efficient (45 students)

These students didn't finish their course or never took the mastery exam. They are stuck in the pipeline — enrolled but never reaching the assessment point. This is a system efficiency failure: time is being spent but the pathway isn't producing measurable outcomes. Why aren't they progressing?

  • Course incompletion — students didn't finish the course and therefore never reached the mastery exam gate
  • Insufficient curriculum pacing — the curriculum didn't move them along fast enough, and no system warned that they were falling behind schedule
  • Insufficient time investment — students may not have spent enough time (expectation: 25 min/day on math); compare to students who did achieve growth
  • Fluency bottleneck — students without HMF=5 in FastMath haven't completed fluency work and may be held back from advancing

Key Patterns

  • Alpha School Austin accounts for 56% (25/45) of this bucket — a campus-specific pacing problem.
  • All 45 students have ZERO test attempts in the Alpha test system — they never reached the assessment gate.
  • 60% are in Math Academy courses, but many are in supplementary/hole-filling tracks (Edia G4 Supp, Edia G6 Supp) that don't have a clear test gateway.
  • Several students show high growth (Anaya Lenington 1.86x, Arman Caberwal 1.83x, C.J. Wright 1.80x) but still didn't take the test — suggesting the pipeline, not the student, is the bottleneck.
  • Outliers like Cade Bustamante (-75x) and Tanner Klentz (-50x) have extreme negative growth values suggesting data anomalies or withdrawal.

Bucket 3a: Tests Not Rigorous Enough (57 students)

These students mastered their effective grade level in 3 or fewer test attempts, passed Alpha tests at 90%+, but still didn't grow 2x on MAP. For K-2 students, our tests are probably not rigorous enough to match MAP's cognitive demands. For grades 3 and up, our tests ARE rigorous — these students genuinely mastered the content but it wasn't enough to move their MAP score. The gap is likely in the depth of grade-level content coverage or the pacing of advancement after mastery.

Key Patterns

  • Efficient test progression: These students passed their effective grade level quickly, suggesting they have foundational competency.
  • K-2 vs Grade 3+ pattern: For K-2, the rigor gap is likely inherent to our assessments. For Grade 3+, these students genuinely mastered content but depth/pacing issues prevent MAP growth.
  • Content coverage gaps: Despite passing Alpha, they may not have covered all standards at the depth MAP requires.

Bucket 3b: Doom Loop (57 students)

These students took 4 or more attempts to pass their effective grade level test. They were likely stuck in a doom loop — cycling through ineffective instructional content (often Freckle or Edia) between failed test attempts, without addressing their foundational knowledge gaps. Key patterns:

  • 47% have HM level ≤ 2, indicating foundational fluency gaps
  • Average time-on-task is 2,408 minutes (vs 2,945 for 3a) — less time despite more attempts
  • 10 students are 3+ grade levels behind their age grade
  • Heavy concentration in Zearn (26) and Math Academy (26) courses
  • Students 3+ grades behind with 5+ attempts show the most severe doom loop patterns

Root cause: These students likely have gaps in foundational knowledge that the system failed to diagnose and address. Each failed attempt wasted time on content that didn't fill the actual gaps.

Key Patterns

  • 4+ attempts is the doom loop threshold: Students retaking the same test multiple times are cycling through content ineffectively.
  • Fluency gaps are prevalent: High proportion of students with HM ≤ 2 suggests foundational arithmetic fluency is a bottleneck.
  • Time efficiency problem: Despite more attempts, these students log fewer hours, indicating rushed, ineffective engagement.
  • Grade placement issue: Students 3+ grades behind in 5+ attempt cycles are misplaced and need diagnostic intervention, not just more content.

Four Systemic Signals

Signal 1: Assessment Validity Crisis

58% of non-growing students PASSED our tests. Internal mastery certification is not translating to external measurement. Alpha tests may measure procedural recall rather than conceptual depth.

Signal 2: Pipeline Efficiency Failure

45 students never took a test. They are stuck in the system — enrolled but never reaching assessment. Completion rates, pacing, or course assignment are breaking down.

Signal 3: Curriculum-Assessment Misalignment

37 students tried and couldn't pass. The curriculum isn't teaching what tests measure, or students are assessed at the wrong level. Many have 3+ attempts — effort without progress.

Signal 4: Doom Loop Cycling

57 students took 4+ attempts on the same test. They are stuck cycling through ineffective instructional tools with undiagnosed foundational gaps. Each attempt wastes time without addressing root causes.

Recommendations by Bucket

Bucket 1: Curriculum Not Effective

  • Audit the gap between curriculum content and test content — if students score 70-89%, targeted gap-filling may close the difference.
  • Review assessment level placement — are students being tested on content they haven't been taught?
  • Implement pre-test diagnostic to identify specific skill gaps before course assignment.

Bucket 2: Curriculum Not Efficient

  • Establish a time-to-test SLA — no student should be in a course for more than a semester without taking the mastery exam.
  • Flag supplementary-only enrollments — students in hole-filling courses without a core pathway are invisible to the mastery system.
  • Create automated alerts when students pass completion thresholds but haven't been assigned a test.

Bucket 3a: Tests Not Rigorous Enough

  • Redesign Alpha tests to include MAP-aligned items — add multi-step reasoning, non-routine problems, and application questions that mirror MAP's cognitive demand.
  • Add a MAP readiness diagnostic layer after Alpha mastery — students who pass Alpha should face a second-tier assessment before being certified grade-level ready.
  • Accelerate curriculum placement — if a student masters G3, immediately move them to G4 rather than letting them plateau.
  • Address the structural grade-level mismatch — passing a below-grade test does not help grow on a grade-level MAP assessment.

Bucket 3b: Doom Loop

  • Doom loop detection system: Alert when any student takes the same grade-level test more than 3 times.
  • Investigate instructional content: When doom loop detected, audit what content they're cycling through (Freckle, Edia, etc.) and why it's not working.
  • Diagnostic-first intervention: Administer a targeted foundational skills diagnostic to identify actual gaps before allowing another test attempt.
  • Replace ineffective content: Pivot to Direct Instruction curriculum or fluency-focused tools for students with HM ≤ 2.
  • Grade placement review: For students 3+ grades behind with 5+ attempts, consider placement correction rather than more attempts.

Solutions: Systemic Fixes to Drive 2x Growth

Actionable recommendations to address curriculum effectiveness, pacing, test rigor, and doom loop detection.

For Bucket 1 & 2: Curriculum Effectiveness & Efficiency

Strengthen Curriculum Effectiveness

  • Adopt curriculum from our new courses: Replace Zearn with Direct Instruction App to provide explicit worked examples and more practice.
  • Audit standards coverage: Ensure all TEKS are covered at the depth and rigor required by MAP assessments.
  • Eliminate low-effectiveness tools: Replace Freckle (K-2) and Edia (3-8) which shows weak outcomes for HF due to lack of instruction.

Enforce Time-on-Task & Pacing Standards

  • Enforce 25 min/day minimum: Students should spend at least 25 minutes daily on math instruction; track and alert on underinvestment.
  • Create pacing alerts: Flag students who haven't progressed in 2 weeks; trigger intervention.
  • Monitor course completion: Students should complete courses within a semester; automate completion checks.

Prerequisite Gates: FastMath Fluency

  • FastMath gating: Students in FastMath must keep pace with main math to finish faster, so implement FastMath Gates in AlphaTest.
  • Fluency bottleneck alert: Flag students stuck at FM HMG less than Math HMG and dedicate time to fluency remediation with a more engaging app.

For Bucket 3a: Assessment Rigor

Improve Test Rigor & Validity

  • K-2 test redesign: Alpha tests for K-2 need higher cognitive demand to match emerging MAP rigor expectations.
  • Grade 3+ monitoring: Our 3-8 tests are already rigorous as evidenced by low pass rates; focus instead on catching doom loops with alerts and ensuring mastery depth with new curriculum.

For Bucket 3b: Doom Loop Prevention

Deploy Doom Loop Detection

  • Alert when any student takes the same grade-level test more than 3 times. This threshold indicates a doom loop.
  • Investigate what instructional content they're cycling through and why it's not working.
  • Replace ineffective content with Direct Instruction or engaging fluency-focused tools.
  • For students 3+ grades behind with 4+ attempts, conduct a comprehensive diagnostic in MA or Incept MA to identify foundational gaps rather than assigning more test attempts.

Foundational Fluency Intervention

  • HMFMG=HMMGrequirement: Students in Bucket 3b with HMG in FM less than HMG in Math should catch up to HMG for M in FM.
  • Fluency bottleneck alert: Flag students stuck without FM=M; change apps and dedicate additional focused time (10 min HW) to fluency remediation.
  • Time-on-task enforcement: These students are logging fewer hours despite more attempts; ensure at least 25 min/day committed to math instruction and 20 to FM practice.

Early Warning System: Dashboard & Alerts

  • Time-on-task vs. growth correlation: Build dashboard showing minutes invested vs. MAP growth; surface students investing time but not growing
  • Automated alerts:
    • Below 25 min/day average over a week
    • No course progress in 10+ days
    • Test attempts > 3 on same grade level
    • High failure count with low time investment (rushing)
    • FM less than M students attempting grade-level work
  • Intervention escalation: Alert → Campus leader review → Student/family outreach within 48 hours

Implementation Priority

  1. Immediately (This week): Replace ineffective and inefficent curriculum for students in buckets 1 and 2
  2. Short-term (Next 2 weeks): Deploy doom loop detection (3+ test attempts alert) and time-on-task monitoring for students in bucket 3b
  3. Medium-term (Next quarter): Replace HF content with MAP-style, TEKS aligned DI instructional content, and refine fluency prerequisites with FastMath mastery gating
  4. Medium-term (Next quarter): Improve K-2 tests with MAP-style, TEKS aligned tests
  5. Ongoing: Monitor alert effectiveness, iterate on intervention response, track 2x growth improvement

Bottom Line for Leadership

196 students failed to reach 2x growth. The data reveals a systemic crisis: 57 students were trapped in doom loops, taking the same test 4+ times while cycling through ineffective content.

  • Bucket 1 (19%, 37 students) — Curriculum Not Effective: Curriculum doesn't teach what tests measure. Fix: Adopt Direct Instruction curriculum, audit TEKS coverage, ensure 25 min/day on-task time.
  • Bucket 2 (23%, 45 students) — Curriculum Not Efficient: Students enrolled but never reach the test. Fix: Implement pacing alerts, enforce semester-based completion SLAs, flag supplementary-only enrollments.
  • Bucket 3a (29%, 57 students) — Tests Not Rigorous Enough: Passed Alpha at 90%+ in 3 or fewer attempts but didn't grow 2x on MAP. Fix: Upgrade K-2 test rigor, add MAP-aligned validation layer for Grade 3+.
  • Bucket 3b (29%, 57 students) — Doom Loop Crisis: Took 4+ attempts on the same test, cycling through Freckle/Edia with undiagnosed gaps. 47% have HM ≤ 2 (fluency issues). Fix: Deploy doom loop detection, diagnostic-first intervention, replace with Direct Instruction for foundational gaps.

The immediate priority: Detect and interrupt doom loops. Then systematically replace low-effectiveness tools and enforce fluency prerequisites. The path forward is not more testing; it's smarter systems, earlier alerts, and deliberate intervention on the patterns that derail growth.
